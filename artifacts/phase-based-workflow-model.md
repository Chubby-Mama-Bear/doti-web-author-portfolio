# Phase-Based Workflow Model

## Purpose

To separate immediate maintenance work from future enhancement work in order to reduce scope confusion and improve execution clarity.

This model was created after identifying that address updates and broader content improvements were being discussed together without structured separation.

---

## Problem Observed

Without phase separation, the workflow risked:

- Mixing urgent updates with long-term improvements
- Expanding scope unintentionally
- Creating planning confusion
- Slowing down execution
- Increasing communication complexity

---

## Proposed Workflow Structure

### Phase 1 — Address Updates

Focus:

Immediate operational maintenance.

Scope included:

- Updating department mailing addresses
- Editing address-related content inside PDFs
- Verifying consistency across documents
- Stabilizing core location-related content

Primary Goal:

Complete essential maintenance work quickly and accurately.

---

### Phase 2 — Content Wording Updates (Future)

Focus:

Content improvement and usability enhancement.

Scope planned to include:

- Updating outdated language
- Improving instruction clarity
- Enhancing content readability
- Standardizing wording structure

Primary Goal:

Improve long-term content quality and usability.

---

## Workflow Logic

Instead of treating all updates as one large project:

Work is divided into controlled stages.

Phase 1 → Stabilize critical information  
Phase 2 → Improve content quality

This sequencing prevents operational overload.

---

## Expected Benefits

### Reduced Scope Confusion

Clear boundaries between maintenance and enhancement work.

---

### Improved Planning Clarity

Teams understand what belongs in each phase.

---

### Faster Execution

Urgent updates are completed without delay from larger improvement discussions.

---

### Better Resource Allocation

Allows future improvements to be planned intentionally rather than reactively.

---

## Operational Significance

This model demonstrates structured workflow thinking by separating immediate operational needs from long-term improvements.

It introduces staged execution, which improves clarity, reduces risk, and supports sustainable content governance.
